Are the Kansas City Chiefs on track to win another Super Bowl in 2024?

football
sports
data
Author

Lauren Aleknavicius

Published

December 2, 2023

The Kansas City Chiefs have appeared in three of the last four Super Bowls. They came away as champions in 2020 and 2023. Although the 2023-2024 regular season is not yet complete, teams are nearing the end of their games. The data is complete through week 12 this season. Currently, the Chiefs are 8-3. This makes them first in their division, the AFC West. The other leaders in the AFC are the Miami Dolphins, Baltimore Ravens, and Jacksonville Jaguars.

Thus, are the Kansas City Chiefs on track to win another Super Bowl in 2024?

The Chiefs are hoping to continue their success into the post season. Unless something drastic happens within the league, the Chiefs will be making their eighth consecutive post season appearance. Much of their success can be contributed to the defense this season. How does the Chiefs defense compare to the other top teams in the AFC?

The Chiefs defense has been keeping the Chiefs on top; however, when comparing rushing and passing yards to the other top teams in the AFC the Chiefs are just average. As of week 12, they were first at stopping passing yards, but last in allowing the most rushing yards.

Code 
library(tidyverse)
library(gt)
library(ggrepel)

afc <- read_csv("defense.csv")

afc |>
  select(
    Team, YdsPass, YdsRush
  ) |> 
  pivot_longer(
  cols=starts_with("Yds"),
  names_to="Type",
  values_to="Yards") -> afcwide 

ggplot()+
  geom_bar(
    data=afcwide,
    aes(x=reorder(Team, Yards),
        weight=Yards, fill= Type)) +
  scale_fill_manual(values=c("orangered1", "orange2")) + 
  coord_flip() +
  
    labs(
    title = "Chiefs Dominate Defensively, Rank 3rd Overall in Yards Per Play.",
    subtitle = "The Jaguars are out for revenge allowing less than 1,000 rushing yards by week 12.",
    caption = "Source: Pro Football Reference | By: Lauren Aleknavicius",
    y= "Defensive Yards Per Play",
    x=""
  ) +
  theme_light() +
  theme(
    plot.title = element_text(size = 16, face ="bold"),
    plot.subtitle = element_text(size = 12),
    axis.title = element_text(size = 8),
    panel.grid.minor = element_blank(),
    plot.title.position = "plot"
  )

The Chiefs offense has been yet to have solid starting game. They have been seeming to pick up speed later this season. Patrick Mahomes has started every game this season at quarterback, unlike the previous season due to an injury. Mahomes is considered one of the best quarterbacks in the league.

Although the AFC West is relatively good at passing this season, there are several teams in the hunt that are not. The Cincinnati Bengals, Tampa Bay Buccaneers, Philadelphia Eagles, and San Francisco 49ers have all appeared in the Super Bowl within the past four seasons. Each team has been a force to be reckon with. On the other hand, The New York Jets and Cleveland Browns have been consistently the worst on offense. Let’s look at passing completion by various teams.

Completed passes and total pass attempts compile together to create a completion percentage. How do the Chiefs compare to the other leaders in the AFC and previous Super Bowl contenders?

All four of the AFC West teams are dominating on offensive and defensive yards per play.

The Jaguars are furthest behind the rest of the pack; however, they are still no where near the worst in the league. With a powerhouse of an offensive and an indestructible defense the Kansas City Chiefs are playing at the top of the league.

Overall, the Kansas City Chiefs are on pace to win another Lombardi Trophy. Their offense is once again unstoppable, but something is different this year. The Chiefs defense is finally stepping up to the plate. With such dominate defense, the Chiefs are in a great position for playing in their second straight Super Bowl.

The Kansas City Chiefs will be a force to be reckon with in the post season in hopes of making another Super Bowl appearance in 2024.
